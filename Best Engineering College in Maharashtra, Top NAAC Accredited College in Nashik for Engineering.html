Our Journey Through Time

1970

Foundation and Early Beginnings

1970: The Karmaveer Kakasaheb Wagh Education Society (KKWES) was established by Padmashree Late Karmaveer Kakasaheb Wagh. The society's initial focus was to provide education to rural communities in the Nashik District, Maharashtra.

1970: The first school was established in Niphad Tehsil, primarily focusing on the education of rural girls.

1983

Focus on Technical Education

1983: K.K. Wagh Polytechnic was founded in Bhausahebnagar, Niphad Tehsil, to cater to the growing demand for skilled technical professionals in the region.

1984: K.K. Wagh College of Engineering was established, marking the society’s foray into higher technical education. Initially located in Bhausahebnagar, the institution was later shifted to Nashik city to accommodate an urban student base.

1993

Women’s Empowerment and Diverse Expansion

1993: Gitai Wagh Kanya Vidyalaya was established to address the need for girls’ education in rural areas.

1996: K.K. Wagh Women's Polytechnic was inaugurated to empower women through specialized technical education programs.

1997-1998: The society ventured into other disciplines, establishing institutions in Arts, Commerce, and Science.

2000

Infrastructure and Growth

2000: The society received 23 acres of land donation from philanthropist Late Kakusheth Udeshi. This became the ‘Hirabai Haridas Vidyanagari’ campus, housing major institutions like the Engineering College, Polytechnic, and Pharmacy Colleges.

2002: Introduction of Agriculture and Computer Science programs, marking a diversified educational portfolio.

2005: Expansion into pharmacy education with the launch of K.K. Wagh College of Pharmacy.

2010

Leadership and National Recognition

2010: KKWES celebrated 40 years of excellence, emphasizing rural development through education.

2015: The engineering institute earned accreditation from the National Board of Accreditation (NBA) and affiliation with Savitribai Phule Pune University.

2020

Golden Jubilee and Present Day

2020: The society celebrated its Golden Jubilee, commemorating 50 years of transformative education.

2021: The total number of institutions reached 35, with programs spanning from kindergarten to doctoral studies.

2023: The society continues to serve over 21,000 students and employs more than 1,900 staff, maintaining its focus on accessible, quality education.

Signature Events at K.K. Wagh College

Ganesha Utsav

This annual celebration brings together students and faculty for a vibrant and culturally rich festival, showcasing art, music, and devotion in a way that reflects the college's spirit of community.

K K Wagh Memorial Cup

A prestigious sports event that celebrates the legacy of K.K. Wagh, bringing together athletes from across various disciplines for a competitive and inspiring tournament.

K K Wagh Guru Shishya

A beautiful event that honors the bond between mentors and students, fostering a sense of respect and admiration for the teaching community.

School Level Competition

An exciting platform for students to showcase their talents in various academic and extracurricular activities, encouraging healthy competition and excellence.

Convocation Ceremony

The convocation ceremony is a momentous occasion where students celebrate the culmination of their hard work and achievements, surrounded by family, faculty, and fellow graduates.

K K Wagh Expo

The K K Wagh Expo provides a platform for students to present their innovative projects and research work to a wider audience, including industry experts and peers.

Equinox

Equinox is an inter-college cultural festival that celebrates creativity, innovation, and talent, featuring performances, competitions, and workshops from across disciplines.

Youth Festival

This annual event brings together youth from various colleges to participate in a variety of cultural and artistic activities, fostering camaraderie and creative expression.

Annual Gathering (Maffic 2023)

The Annual Gathering is one of the most awaited events, with performances, award ceremonies, and an overall grand celebration of the college's achievements and student life.
